Acupuncture Care in Calgary

Acupuncture treatments and TCM are designed to support your body’s natural ability to heal and regulate. Whether you are dealing with pain, digestive concerns, or ongoing stress, care is tailored to your specific needs.

The goal is not only to provide relief, but to help you understand your health in a way that allows you to maintain it independently. Treatment is aimed not only at the symptoms, but at education and giving you the tools to manage your well-being with less reliance on ongoing care.

How I Treat

I provide acupuncture in Calgary using a structured, analytical approach rooted in Traditional Chinese Medicine to identify patterns and restore balance in the body.

Treatments may include acupuncture, herbal formulas, and lifestyle guidance tailored specifically to your condition and constitution.

My goal is not only to relieve symptoms, but to address the underlying imbalance, and support long-term health that you can sustain on your own.

About

Acupuncturist • TCM Herbalist • Chinese Metaphysics Enthusiast

Hi, I'm Tammy.

I began my career in accounting after earning my Commerce degree from UBC. After obtaining my CPA, I stepped away from work to raise my family.

During that time, my baby developed severe eczema. Traditional Chinese Medicine was what resolved it — and that experience changed my path completely.

I went on to study at the Alberta College of Acupuncture and TCM, and for over 10 years I’ve been serving Calgarians as a practitioner, educator, and volunteer acupuncturist.

I take a structured, analytical approach to treatment and focus on pain, migraines, and digestive health.

Outside the clinic, I enjoy time with my kids and friends, cooking, knitting, and studying TCM foundations and Chinese Metaphysics.

Services

Acupuncture

$90 initial (75 min) • $60 follow-up (45 min)

Includes TCM, Balance Method, Master Tung, auricular, and motor point release. To maximize your needle retention time, it's best to wear loose-fitting clothes that can be rolled up to your elbows and knees. A loose tank top or shorts may be helpful. Sometimes it's not good to have acupuncture done. If you are too weak or too hungry, acupuncture will be advised against. It's best to have something to eat an hour or so before acupuncture. If you are on blood-thinners, acupuncture may not be for you. While I do see many women's health issues, my treatments tend to be a bit too moving so I do not treat IVF-fertility or while pregnant - you can see the lovely folks at https://www.wellnesson1st.com (1st St/16 Ave NW) , Cheryl Lee (clwilliams4@gmail.com - Nolan Hill), or Sarah Kim (sarahkimcalgary@gmail.com - downtown core) for this care.

Telehealth

$90 initial (45 min) • $30 follow-up (15 min)

Don't have time to come in to get your pulse read? You can send in other information at least 24 hours in advance of your telehealth appointment, which includes lots of detail on the intake and progress forms, pictures of your tongue and ears, and ideally blood pressure readings. Your information will be reviewed and the telehealth appointment will be to confirm the diagnosis. You then can visit herb shops to buy OTC herbs, or if you wish to purchase herbs from me, your herbs will be mailed out to you. To get the most out of telehealth appointments, it would be best to invest in a blood pressure monitor so you can send that information as well. (Costco has very decent and reasonably priced digital blood pressure monitors. I have no affiliation with Costco.)

TCM Herbal Formulas

$30 / 50g - taken over approx 7-10 days

I use Classical TCM formulas in granule form - a dissolvable powder which you add to a cup of hot water and stir, much like how instant coffee is prepared. While less potent than raw herbs, the granules taste much better and are much less work, a very welcome innovation in this ancient tradition. I use brands that have Health Canada NPNs to meet Health Canada's standards for endangered species and contamination/toxicity.

Practice Information

A simple, patient-focused practice

I run a small, independent practice where I manage all aspects of care — from treatment to administration. This allows me to keep things simple and focus fully on you during your visit.

At this time, I do not offer direct billing to third-party insurance providers. Receipts will be provided so you can submit to your insurance company for reimbursement or use for tax purposes.

Most insurance plans cover acupuncture, however herbal medicine and telehealth appointments are often not included. To avoid disappointment, please check with your provider in advance.

Payment methods: E-transfer and cash accepted.
