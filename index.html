Acupuncture Care in Calgary

Acupuncture treatments and TCM are designed to support your body’s natural ability to heal and regulate. Whether you are dealing with pain, digestive concerns, or ongoing stress, care is tailored to you.

The goal is not only to provide relief, but to help you understand your health in a way that allows you to maintain it independently. Treatment is aimed not only at the symptoms, but at education and giving you the tools to manage your well-being with less reliance on ongoing care.

Targeted Care for Pain, Migraines, and Digestive Health

Pain

Migraines

Digestive (IBS)

Many patients seek acupuncture for ongoing pain, migraines, and digestive concerns such as IBS and reflux. These conditions are often persistent, and can significantly affect daily life.

Treatment focuses on identifying the underlying pattern contributing to your symptoms, rather than addressing each issue in isolation. This allows care to be both more precise and more effective over time.

Whether you are dealing with chronic pain, recurring migraines, or digestive imbalance, the goal is to reduce symptom frequency and intensity while improving overall stability.

How I Treat

I use a structured, analytical approach rooted in Traditional Chinese Medicine to identify patterns and guide treatment. Each visit is focused, intentional, and adapted to how your condition is presenting at that time.

Treatment may include acupuncture, herbal formulas, and practical guidance that you can apply outside of the clinic. The goal is not only to create change during the session, but to support progress between visits.

Care is designed to move you toward greater stability and independence, so that over time, you are less reliant on ongoing treatment and more confident in managing your own health.

Services

Acupuncture

$90 initial (75 min) • $60 follow-up (45 min)

Common reasons for seeking treatment include pain conditions, migraines, and digestive issues such as IBS and reflux.

Treatment may incorporate Traditional Chinese Medicine, Balance Method, Master Tung, auricular acupuncture, and motor point release, depending on your presentation, treatment is guided by how the body is functioning as a whole.

To support effective treatment, it is recommended to arrive having eaten lightly within a few hours and wearing loose-fitting clothing that allows access to the arms and legs.

In some cases, acupuncture may not be appropriate (for example, if you are very fatigued, have not eaten, or are taking certain medications such as blood thinners). While I do see many women's health issues, my treatments tend to be a bit too moving so I do not treat IVF-fertility or while pregnant - you can see the lovely folks at https://www.wellnesson1st.com (1st St/16 Ave NW) , Cheryl Lee (clwilliams4@gmail.com - Nolan Hill), or Sarah Kim (sarahkimcalgary@gmail.com - downtown core) for this care.

Telehealth

$90 initial (45 min) • $30 follow-up (15 min)

Don't have time to come in to get your pulse read? You can send in other information at least 24 hours in advance of your telehealth appointment, which includes lots of detail on the intake and progress forms, pictures of your tongue and ears, and ideally blood pressure readings. Your information will be reviewed and the telehealth appointment will be to confirm the diagnosis. You then can visit herb shops to buy OTC herbs, or if you wish to purchase herbs from me, your herbs will be mailed out to you. To get the most out of telehealth appointments, it would be best to invest in a blood pressure monitor so you can send that information as well. (Costco has very decent and reasonably priced digital blood pressure monitors. I have no affiliation with Costco.)

TCM Herbal Formulas

$30 / 50g - taken over approx 7-10 days

I use Classical TCM formulas in granule form - a dissolvable powder which you add to a cup of hot water and stir, much like how instant coffee is prepared. While less potent than raw herbs, the granules taste much better and are much less work, a very welcome innovation in this ancient tradition. I use brands that have Health Canada NPNs to meet Health Canada's standards for endangered species and contamination/toxicity.

Practice Information

A simple, patient-focused practice

I run a small, independent practice where I manage all aspects of care — from treatment to administration. This allows me to keep things simple and focus fully on you during your visit.

At this time, I do not offer direct billing to third-party insurance providers. Receipts will be provided so you can submit to your insurance company for reimbursement or use for tax purposes.

Most insurance plans cover acupuncture, however herbal medicine and telehealth appointments are often not included. To avoid disappointment, please check with your provider in advance.

Payment methods: E-transfer and cash accepted.

PILSC appointments at MREC

Help that's closer to home - Program access - Fundraising

PILSC community members are able to access acupuncture free of charge at this office. Sign up at www.pilsc.org and visit the centre for your initial appointment, the next 7 visits can then be booked at MREC.

The program is open to 2 (two) PILSC community members at a time for weekly appointments at MREC, to be completed over an 8 week period, or ended sooner if you wish. All appointments must be completed within 8 weeks, any missed appointments are not able to be made up outside of the 8 weeks. Your health information will be in the PILSC system, not this office's system.

After the 8 week period is complete, the spot will be given to the next person interested. You are welcome to join the waitlist. If there is no one on the waitlist then you may continue until another PILSC community member is interested in acupuncture at this location.

Scheduling: Scheduling will be arranged at your initial visit as two scheduling systems will need to be updated. Be sure to ask about scheduling at MREC.

Fees: Pay what you want! Services range from $0 to $70 (or pay what you want). You will receive a receipt that can be submitted to your insurance for reimbursement. Any fees collected go toward keeping the acupuncture program running at the Pregnancy & Infant Loss Support Centre location in the south.

Payment methods: Any fees you wish to give will be collected from the credit card on file through PILSC's online booking system.
